# Visual Density Prototype 01 — Final Prompt Set

All sheets were produced with the built-in image generation tool. A flat `#00ff00` background was requested for local alpha extraction.

## LOW — simplified

```text
Use case: stylized-concept
Asset type: KeyBuddy low-resolution game character prototype sheet
Primary request: create a deliberately simple LOW-RES pixel-art barbarian in three clearly separated full-body poses: neutral idle holding one small axe in each hand; tapping his chest once with his anatomical right axe hand while still gripping the axe; a clear dual-axe attack impact pose.
Subject: muscular copper-skinned adult male barbarian, black braided hair, short black beard, bare torso, one large symbolic dark tattoo across the anatomical right arm and side, a small iron guard on his anatomical right shoulder fixed by one diagonal leather harness, simple bracers, dark fur trousers and boots, two matching one-handed axes.
Style/medium: true compact low-resolution game sprite design, about 48x64 logical pixels per character, three-head-tall stylized proportions, large readable head and hands, chunky silhouette, very limited 10-to-14 color palette, one-step highlights and shadows, thick one-logical-pixel outline, large clean pixel clusters, no micro-detail.
Composition/framing: three equal-size figures aligned on the same ground line, generous separation and padding; consistent head size, body height, proportions, costume and axes across poses; front-biased three-quarter view looking toward screen-right.
Scene/backdrop: perfectly flat solid #00ff00 chroma-key background for removal, one uniform color only.
Constraints: must remain clearly readable at around 120-to-200 screen pixels tall; crisp hard pixel edges; no anti-aliasing; no blur; no text; no labels; no shadows; no floor; no extra props; do not use #00ff00 in the character; exactly two axes in every pose.
Avoid: realistic anatomy rendering, detailed muscle striations, small tattoos, detailed fur strands, painterly shading, soft gradients, noisy pixels, inconsistent scale, cropped weapons, extra limbs, extra weapons, watermark.
```

Correction applied afterward:

```text
Change only the shoulder-armor placement in all three figures: the small iron shoulder guard must be on the barbarian's anatomical right shoulder, which appears on screen-left for this front-biased three-quarter view. The diagonal leather harness must visibly secure that guard across the chest. Remove the incorrect guard from the screen-right shoulder. Preserve everything else exactly.
```
